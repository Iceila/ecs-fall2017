Composition


WHAT IS COMPOSITION?

First of all, if you made it here, congratulations. You should have mastered or at least become proficient in all the previous fundamentals. Composition should be the last thing to add to your skill set.

By this point, you should be able to draw originally. This fundamental will help you improve upon your art.

Composition helps make your art more pleasing to the human eye. There are certain proportions that humans find attractive. For example, the golden ratio, which is present in nature, the human face, and, if used correctly, can make your art look pleasing and natural.

Composition is used in logo design, art, and when shooting movies. It’s all around you, if you know where to look. If you’re looking to become a professional artist, composition is a must.

HOW TO LEARN COMPOSITION

Now that you’ve mastered the most fundamentals of fundamentals, composition isn’t too hard to learn. Research about natural or aesthetic patterns, like the Rule of Thirds or the Golden Ratio. Then figure out how to apply all this to your art. This can be adding a subtle curve to something in the background, in order to draw your eye to the subject of the image.

Composition can be used with colors as well. Like we said in the colors fundamental, light areas draw the eye.

Challenge: Combine all the fundamentals you’ve learned into one big masterpiece. It can be landscape, figure, abstract, or anything you want!
